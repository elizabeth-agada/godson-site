Introduction

Over the past twenty years, the world has seen amazing technological advancements. We are able to connect with each other and conduct business more efficiently than ever before. This is all largely due to the open sharing of data.

BHere in Granada and across the Caribbean, data sharing has been significantly slow to catch on, leaving our public systems and businesses unable to keep up with the global development around us.

CariData provides a simple way to store and access data across the Caribbean region. It will help foster growth for small businesses needing to tap into data sources, simplify data collection and record management, and boost the ICT sector by giving our developers access to an open data system upon which to build. A Caribbean data revolution is long overdue, and CariData is here to guide us through it.

The problem

Most of our public records in Granada are either fully manual (written in paper and stored in filing cabinets) or are managed in a soiled computer system.

We believe that the lack of open data with which to build functional software programs is proving to be a hindrance to our growth as a nation.

The Solution

Application programmable interfaces - better known as API's - are commonly used to give developers, researchers and investors access to large amounts of data in a format they can view, search and filter.

Custom filing system that allows eaasy access to the data for updates, and to ensure data integrity.

In the long-term, we propose the consolidation of all pertinent public data throughout the Caribbean to help drive development and efficiency.
