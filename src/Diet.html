Overview

Diet and food recipe app, personalized to the individual's tastes. It provides strict or casual dieters with a range of personalised recommendations to improve cooking skills, healthy eating habits or weight loss journey by providing a variety of nutritous and suitable meals recipe.

The brief

Malnutrition, in all its forms, is a critical global public health problem. Increasingly, the conditions of undernutrition and overweight, obesity and diet-related noncommunicable diseases (NCDs) coexist in nations, communities and households. Similarly, overweight, obesity and diet-related NCDs- which are increasing in children and adults, especially in urban populations-result in premature mortality and the early onset of disease with high levels of disability.

In 2015, more than 1.9 billion adults were overweight or obese worldwide, while 462 million were underweight. In 2016, over 340 million children aged 5-19 years were overweight or obese, while 192 milloin were underweight. Furthermore, in 2017, 151 million children aged under 5 years were affected by stunting, while 38 million were overweight and 51 million were affected by wasting.

Design Process

We followed the Double Diamond approach because it enables self awareness where discovery and definition work is lacking. It also allowed us to map and understand the problems, so we know what to do next and what tools are available to use.

Discover

We started by learning about the different variables that affect the problem and its possible solution. It was important for us to to start by laying down the problem, presenting our hypothesis, and defining ways we can learn more from the users. To effectively solve the problem we needed to identify and contextualise the actual problem or opportunity..

User Research

The goal of this study is to understand the major pain points that people who are on a casual or strict diet plan experience, people who did not follow a diet before but want to change this or people who are already used to a specific diet as well as the limitations of the existing platforms they use. We also wanted to learn about the local names and sources of healthy food in the area.

We will also employ generative research techniques to gain a better understanding of our customers' experiences with our brand as well as the diet challenges and needs they face.

Design

Modular Design System

When planning a digital product for a long project, it is important to constantly ask yourself two question: "How long will it last?" and "is it scalable?" we began by creating a reusable components library, which would eventually be reflected in many aspects of the app, such as colours, font, grid settings, and so on. Icons, buttons, form elements, error and empty states, display cards, and content card were all provided. We used a 12pt grid technique to achieve vertical rhythm.

Diet.ng was created with scalability in mind, so that components can operate together and render well on a variety of device resolutions.
