Overview

Boltzmenn was hoping to redesign their website to highlight and also incorporate their new objectives and emphasize the services they provide to African businesses and industry-related startups. The project will cover concept and design.

Buddhist meditation practice has a lot of benefits, such as:finding inner peace, helping to increase focus and attention, fight stress, be more creative, and so on.

However, for some people it's not always possible to go to meditation centers or classes to attend sessions. We wanted to understand what are the challenges around the practice of meditation that people have, and create an experience to solve those challenges.

The Brief

Boltzmenn current website doesn't reflect its dynamic mission. Aesthetically, it was visual dumping ground that didn't do justice to the brand's purpose. There was no structure to the content. It amplified confusion rather than coherency.

The challenges was developing a UX strategy that targets users with real value and implementing these solutions into a website experience.

About the Company

Boltzmenn is a creative agency based in Lagos, Nigeria. An agency of brand experts, strategic researchers, idea generators. They help businesses around Africa solve brand, marketing, communictions, and product challenges.

A strong digital presence is key in their highly competitive environment. Boltzmenn doesn't think like most companies. The whole ethos, from their inception years ago, is based on building a better tomorrow for business through challenging conventions.

It needed some restructuring and a visual boost to take it to the next level. That is exactly what i have tried to do in this project.

Let's get into it.

Solution

The best way to start cooking up anything new is by taking a look at your ingredients with a content audit. Poor URL mapping, an ineffective site map.

That content audit also gave us a clearer picture of what we needed to create and design.

Sitemap and Wireframs were the next step, to define information hierarchy, plan layout, map a more efficient navigation flow and create functionality based on how we wanted uses to process the information.

Sitemap

Visual Design

Started collecting visual ideas, words and images. Intitially we had a simple word sequence based on our conversations with client and a mood board with relevant designs and ideas. The main visual features we were after were simplicity, bold typography, nice photos and clean icons.

Ignoring the urge to follow certain trends just because everybody else is doing it. Creating a thorough mood board of relevant reference designs that approximate the look and feel we're going after. This was in line with our goals and target audience.

Key Learnings

Time Management

Using time boxing for almost all tasks throughout the project. This kept me on track and enabled me to efficently move forward at each stage of the project.

Communication

At the first day of every week, I Prioritized a 25-minute scrum to discuss with a representative from the agency, where we sorted any concerns we had and what we intended to proceed with for the week. We maintained these weekly and it was extremely valuable in establishing open Communication within the agency.

Footnote

Looking back at the website redesign process, it's apparent that the majority of time was dedicated to organizing and planning. Only 30 percent of the project was spent designing the site.

When you embark on this kind of journey, make sure you're in good company. Many great projects are about teams and there are always too many people to thank for but I wanted to highlight few people who made this project happen.
