Overview

With more than 33.9 million Nigerians scrolling through their feed (according to Statista ) which makes Nigeria the largest number of social media users in Africa Since the pandemic started, internet penetration and social media use has reached new highs, with people pushing out new content in places like TikTok, instagram, and Facebook. This also has led to the increase of social influencers.

Social media influencers in Nigeria communicate with their clients via email, DMs on their preferred social media platforms, or phone calls. This can make it difficult for clients or brands to keep track of their social media campaigns with these influencers. There is a need for a centralized system where both influencers and brands can track their campaigns and make changes as needed.

The aim of this project was to connect influencers, content creators, and media managers in the digital space with ground-level entrepreneurs and brands. The sole objective was to make influencer marketing straightforward for both clients and influencers in Nigeria.

My Role

My role with them was to lead the user experience and interface design of their flagship product.

My job was to create a scalable and end to end UX vision that align well with their business model. Although the product has expanded and changed, I am always proud to be associated with the start of their journey.

The Problem

Working with influencers whose content already resonates with your brand. Finding partnership that allows influencers to stay true to their image whilst adhering to the brief. Researching and recording which influencers are suitable for their purposes using tools.

Measuring the ROI of influencer marketing is challenging. Viewing the content and creator performance metrics, compiling all the usual suspects like impressions, reach, views, and engagement rate, as well as influencer's very own impact rate.

Work Process

My team worked in an agile manner, running increamental sprints. We had daily standups to resolve issues and blockers as we had a limited time to build out the MVP of the project.

Competitive Audit

There are quite a few influencer marketing platforms out there-- I chose and compared the two most well-known major competitors being Grin and Upfluence whichoffers similar services and business models. More Findings

Upfluence

It is an influencer marketing platform that allows you to find the top influencers, monitor campaigns, and generate more lead sales through authentic influencer partnerships.

Grin

Is an influencer marketing platform, designed to connect Brands with 'influencers' who are willing to post the brands on their chosen social media platforms for a fee per post.

Insight

After conducting countless user interviews, contextual inquiry and analyzing the gathered data, I was able to categorize the insights into these categories.

Identification of influencers

  • Brands are constanly looking for the 'RIGHT' type of influencers.
  • Filter based search for reputable influencers.
  • Verification and screening process.

Inbound Identification:

  • DMs
  • Emails
  • Third party agencies

Communication

  • Straight away, inbound emails and DMs are messy, they get lost in all the other noise.
  • Brands and influencers needs strong collaborations to achieve a win-win situation for both parties.
  • Third party agencies provides little value and yet they take the biggestslice of the pie.

Outbound Identification:

With respect to outbound, brands can browse social media platforms to find creators who best match their guidelines but realistically this isn't very efficient - social media platforms don't allow you to search for creators via different attributes or qualities.

End-to-end process management.

  • User interface of competitors are more "sales centered" rather than "user centered".
  • Banner Blindness - Audiences are used to the positioning and look of the ads, and they usually ignore them.
  • Measuring ROI continues to be one of the biggest pain point in influencer marketing.

Project Learnings

Simplicity is strength

As a designer, we are often lured by attractive, trendy and out of the box designs. But, we must always remember the 'why.' The primary goal is to understand the user, their problems and then come up with a design that solves it.

Prioritize

Create a strategic plan to launch an MVP. This helps deal with out-of-scope requests that could potentially derail the project and helps deliver a quality product in time.

Seek out feedback early and continually

The trouble with most of us is that we would rather be ruined by praise than saved by criticism. Keeping the stakeholders/users in loop and testing solutions in whatever form (paper, low-fi or hi-fi) as early as possible saves ample amount of time and re-work.
