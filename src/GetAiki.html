Overview

As a result of global pandemic and digital revolution, there is a high demand for talents (freelancers) and a lot of business owners are looking for more hands to get work done. The software ecosystem places a high value on freelancing since it allows businesses to outsource jobs to meet their needs for digital transformation.

The freelance market is on the rise, helped by the pandemic that compels most firms to become digital to maintain their revenue streams. With the great resignation, it is predicted that freelance demand will continue to rise after COVID-19.

However, the IT freelancing market still doesn't recieve enough attention. Getaiki now has the opportunity to address the void and offer a better experience for students that wants to freelance while schooling.

The Goal

To give business owners quick access to vetted freelancers and provide student freelancers with a premium platform to sell their gigs with competitive price.

My Role

As the sole designer, I worked closely with the company's founder, one Product Manager and the software Engineering Team. I lead the design of the product from start to finish and created the prototype for both the business and admin platform.

Getting off the ground and applying the design process

While starting the project I reminded myself and other team members what the project owner want: to make their app functional and usable beyond their competitors; and increase trust between business owners and freelancers

Usability Testing & Iterations

The next step was the testing stage to see the chance for improvements, as regards the usability of the platform. It was a virtual one over Google meets.

  • Lo-fi prototypes were tested with the stakeholders weekly to get feedback on the functionality, content, and interactivity of the product.
  • Unmoderated User testing - A few dummy groups were created for both influencers and brand marketers to use the app. All participants were using the app to carry ou hypothetical tasks.

Project Learnings

Simplicity and Strength

As designers, we are often lured by attractive, trendy and out of the box designs. But, we must always remember the 'why.' The primary goal is to understand the user, their problems and then come up with a design that solves it.

Prioritize

Create a strategic plan to launch an MVP. This helps deal with out-of-scope requests that could potentially derail the project and helps deliver quality product in time.

Seek out feedback early and continually

The trouble with most of us is that we would rather be ruined by praise than saved by critism. Keeping the stakeholders/users in loop and testing solutions in whatever form (paper, low-fi or hi-fi) as early as possible saves ample amount of time and re-work.
